The brief

Get found by people who didn't know they were looking yet.

TikTok works as a search engine for high-intent buyers now. Type a real question, get a real answer, often from a real person. The brief was to build a content engine that ranks for the queries an aviation-curious audience is genuinely typing. No ads. The right answer, in the right format.

"Learn to fly Spain". "How much does a helicopter licence cost". "How long does training take". Each query becomes its own short film, answered by a pilot, on the day, on the airfield.

  • Decision-stage queries
  • Pilot-led answers
  • Search-friendly titles
  • Stacked video families
In the wild

Three of the reels that did the work.

How it ran

Three moves. Thirty days. The right kind of reach.

01

Find the queries.

Mapped every high-intent question a prospective student types into TikTok and Google before they pick up the phone. Not the easy ones. The decision-stage ones.

  • Cost queries — "how much does a licence cost"
  • Duration queries — "how long does helicopter training take"
  • Location queries — "learn to fly Spain"
  • Safety and age queries — the ones people quietly Google
02

Answer them on camera.

Each query becomes its own short film. A pilot, on the airfield, answering the question in plain English. No script readers. No corporate gloss.

  • One question, one answer, one cut
  • Founder and instructor on camera
  • Search-friendly titles, captions, on-screen text
03

Stack the watch time.

Multiple videos targeting the same query family. The algorithm rewards depth, the buyer rewards consistency.

  • Series, not one-offs
  • Cross-link in captions and replies
  • Reuse the strongest cuts on Reels and Shorts

Sixty-eight thousand views in the first month. Thirty days, no spend, no guesswork. Just the right answer in the right format.
